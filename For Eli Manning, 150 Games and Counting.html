For Eli Manning, 150 Games and Counting
By MIKE BOSTOCK, SHAN CARTER and KEVIN QUEALY
On Sunday, Manning will start his 150th consecutive game for the Giants. Below, how his streak compares to the streaks of nearly 500 quarterbacks who have started a game since 1970. Related article
Each streak shows consecutive starts by a quarterback for a single team. Streaks include playoffs.
Only two players have longer streaks: Brett Favre (275) and Elis brother, Peyton (227).
Among active players, Philip Rivers (122) and Joe Flacco (96) are closest behind Eli.

The players strike of the 1987 season ended a handful of established streaks, including those of Dan Marino and John Elway.
On the other end of the spectrum are the quarterbacks who have bounced around the league: Gus Frerotte and Steve Beuerlein started games for seven different teams each, the most of any quarterback shown here.
Of active players, Byron Leftwich has started with more teams than any of his peers: with the Jaguars, Falcons, Steelers and Bucaneers.
